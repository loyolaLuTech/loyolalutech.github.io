loyola hacks for greatness ++

when?

April 2nd, 2016 | 10am - 5pm | Cuneo 116 (LSC)

what?

This event will have individuals or pairs of programmers (of all skill backgrounds!) coming together to solve enough programming problems to bring Loyola into the Top 50 Universities IN THE WORLD) on the popular competitive programming site, Open Kattis. There are problems to tackle from very simple ("introduction-to-programming-style") to moderately difficult ("you-must-be-a-pretty-good-programmer-style"), to incredibly complex ("have-you-considered-a-PhD"). Come learn a thing or two about coding (whether you're mostly new to programming or a seasoned veteran) and join the fun as Loyola Hacks for Greatness! Food will be provided!

who?

Everyone. Undergraduates, graduates, faculty, and staff. The entire Loyola family is welcome. Very mild programming experience is necessary, we will be solving a sample problem together to help you along! Come one, come all, and show us your chops!

Sign up by clicking here!

Loyola Hacks for Greatness will focus on solving as many problems as we can as a school - programmers can work on their own or in pairs, and whoever scores the most points (points are awarded based on difficulty), wins both glory for our university, and tangible prizes from us!

Prizes will be awarded, and will be oh-so-nerdy. Sign up for the event to find out more!
