loyola hacks entrepreneurship

when?

April 23rd, 2016 | 10am - 7pm | Cuneo 311 (LSC)

what?

A competition of technology startup ideas at Loyola. This event is for anyone with a tech-minded business idea, anyone with software development skills who wants to develop apps and get a taste of startup culture, or anyone curious about what it takes to work on a startup idea - to rapidly develop and pitch a business idea, a business plan, and show off a prototype of a tech product in a fast and competitive setting.

who?

Everyone. Undergraduate and Graduate students of any department with an interest in startup creation. No computer programming experience is necessary, though it would not hurt.

Loyola Hacks Entrepreneurship will have teams will be working to pitch a business plan and showcase a prototype of an app or technology project to a panel of faculty judges. Any ideas have a chance to win the competition, as long as they are well-designed and presented. The entire event will take place over 8 hours in one day. Fully-realized business plans and products are not expected. The best projects will be those that can clearly pitch and show off their idea in the most interesting and realistic way. This is a competition! Winners will be judged by professors who have worked in industry from the Computer Science Department and School of Business. Prizes and food will be provided!
